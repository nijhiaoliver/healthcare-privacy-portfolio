## Project Overview

This audit analyzes Change Healthcare's (CHC) privacy notice across three time periods:
- **Pre-Breach** — Notice in effect prior to February 21, 2024
- **Post-Breach** — HIPAA Substitute Notice and breach communications (2024)
- **Current State** — Optum's absorbed privacy policy (last updated August 11, 2025)

Change Healthcare, a healthcare clearinghouse and business associate to hundreds of covered entities, suffered a ransomware attack on February 21, 2024 — one of the largest healthcare data breaches in U.S. history, ultimately affecting an estimated **192.7 million individuals**.

This audit evaluates whether CHC's privacy notices met regulatory requirements before, during, and after the breach — and proposes a model notice that reflects what adequate disclosure should look like for a clearinghouse of this scale.

### Post-Breach Substitute Notice Findings

**Source:** https://www.changehealthcare.com/hipaa-substitute-notice
**Notice Date:** June 2024 (initial posting)

**Summary Finding:**

> The substitute notice describes the timeline of the breach in detail but relies on vague language regarding remediation — stating CHC "reinforced its policies and practices and implemented additional safeguards" without specifying what those safeguards were or how they address the identified vulnerabilities. The notice does not describe the root cause of the breach or acknowledge any systemic security failures.

**Notable Gaps:**
- [ ] No disclosure of root cause (lack of MFA on legacy systems cited by OCR)
- [ ] Remediation language is non-specific and unverifiable
- [ ] No mention of structural/architectural changes
- [ ] Categories of PHI listed as potentially impacted but not confirmed until late in review
- [ ] SUD/behavioral health data implications not addressed
